Projects

  • Dynamics-informed surrogates: Autoencoder-NeuralODEs for reduced-order chemical kinetics

    A physics-informed autoencoder-neural ODE framework is developed for data-driven modeling of stiff chemical kinetics, wherein autoencoder is employed for dimensionality reduction of the thermochemical state and the neuralODE learns the temporal evolution of the dynamical system in the latent space. Paper

  • ML based wall models for aero-thermal simulations

    A priori analysis of ML methods is carried out with the goal of data-driven wall modeling of large eddy simulation (LES) of gas turbine film cooling flows. Parametric tests are conducted to systematically assess the influence of a wide range of input flow features on the accuracy of ML wall model with respect to prediction of wall shear stress. In addition, the use of spatial stencil and time delay is also explored within the ML wall modeling framework. Paper

  • Pareto-efficient framework for optimized reduced-order model assignment in reacting flows

    A modeling framework for reacting flows was developed to adaptively switch between finite-rate chemistry in limited local regions where it is necessary, and low-cost approaches, such as flamelet-type models wherever applicable. Paper

  • Randomized forward gradients on AI accelerators

    Explored the applicability and efficacy of forward gradients, where an unbiased estimator for the gradient is computed in a single forward sweep to train a machine leaning wall model for CFD application. Paper

  • Eulerian thermo-mechanical simulations of propellants

    Developed a 3D computational framework for strain augmented burning of solid propellants, using a Chorin-type projection approach to deal with extreme material dissimilarity. Paper
